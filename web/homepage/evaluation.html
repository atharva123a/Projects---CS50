IQ BEFORE IQ


The concept of IQ was introduced in 1912, so how was intelligence evaluated before?


Behavorial Intelligence and it's Significance.



Historically, even before IQ tests were devised, there were attempts to classify people into intelligence categories by observing their behavior in daily life.Those other forms of behavioral observation are still important for validating classifications based primarily on IQ test scores. Both intelligence classification by observation of behavior outside the testing room and classification by IQ testing depend on the definition of "intelligence" used in a particular case and on the reliability and error of estimation in the classification procedure.



Contribution of psychologists


The early developments in the field of measuring intelligence were taken by people who were interested in the human brain and the way external simulations such as any form of brain activity and the results were based on reflexes, precision and speed at which these tasks were executed. In fact even today these fundamentals are crucial for any IQ test to be standardized. The early scientists experiments thought of intelligence to be dependent on head size, reflexes and genetics. However, when they were actually asked to set up equipments and prove the accuracy of these criterias, they weren't were successful and thus there was no standard IQ test as such, making it difficult to standardize this notion of intelligence. Among many contributors the major contributors were: Francis Galton, Alfred Binet, Robert Yerks and David Wechsler (a lot of other psychologists have not being mentioned). The works of all these psychologists have helped create standard tests that could be applied to majority of the students.
