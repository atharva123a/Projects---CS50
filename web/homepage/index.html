IQ: Intelligence Quotient...


IQ stands for Intelligence Quotient and is used to denote the score derieved from a set of standardized tests carried out to judge human intelligence based on a set of tests. This score gained immense popularity as a tool for juding the mental performance of a person based on his mental age. Prior to the concept of IQ there were different ways in which people measured intellegience in the historic times.



A Brief History Of Intelligence Tests


The term IQ was coined by psychologist William Stern for the German term Intelligenzquotient, his term for a scoring method for intelligence tests at University of Breslau he advocated in a 1912 book.

In the historic times, behavior was a major factor while judging the intelligence of children. These behavioral attributes for judging the intelligence of children are still considered, however the importance that these had have changed over time. Children these days are judged in terms of emotional intelligence more than behavioral intelligence and thus to even out the judgement for intelligence we now give more emphasis to these IQ tests. There were a lot of psychologists that helped in developing the IQ test standards that we have today.



IQ Scores and Evaluation


If you have read the times when tests were evaluated based on behavorial intelligence and were not standardized, the major concern was people who gave the tests could have different emotional responses to different situations and this becomes more subjective than objective. This also means that the tests cannot judge the intelligence of all people irrespective of where they come from. Thus, modern tests were developed which have tried to give equal opportunities to people from a all walks of life. The tests now pose questions which challenge the cognitive and reasoning abilities of the person which are more objective and can be compared making it easier for evaluation. The evaluation is done by using a bell curve (as in normal distribution curve) which in fact was used in the early times by psychologists such as Francis Galton to plot and figure out the variation in intelligence across generations.

Is Your IQ really THAT Important?

Let's take a look...

When we take a closer look at IQ tests we would figure out soon that these are more or less imperfect. They try to judge the intellectual ability of humans by creating certain puzzles that challenging their cognitive ability and forcing them to use certain parts of their brains. The way the scores are given are based on how fast and accurate the answers to all the questions attempted are given. This, however, does not gaurantee that the person displays the same qualitites while making decisions, or when put in curcumstances which are not ideal
The reason these tests are not perfect, is because they do not test the creativity, emotional intelligence, social skills and even wisdom. These points are actually massively underrated, but are very important infact, even more than your IQ. There are times when people with better emotional intelligence will be better at decision making than another person who is weak emotionally, but has a higher IQ score. The learning of facts faster, is great, but what is more interesting is what you can do with the knowledge that you have accumulated. There are a lot of evidences that higher IQ is not equal to success and that is also fairly visible considering that the people who do good in schools do not always do great in their lives.
