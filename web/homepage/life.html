Intelligence is Abstract


When you look at children, the way we analyze their intelligence is based on how creative they are, or how perspicacious they can be while doing certain tasks. But, what really makes us curious is how quick-witted they can be at times. These certainly are a few factors through which we might try to judge the intelligence of kids. But, at a certain point they are all asked to give the IQ tests to "standardize" their intelligence. This is actually the first step to kill someone's creativity. The reason these tests are so popular is that they give you a number, a number which you can use to rank your kid among the vast population in this world. You are subconsciously trying to make sure that your kid is competent, in a race which is itself incompetent to test your kid's competence. The reason kids who do great in school do not always do great in life is that they basically are really competitive at this incompetent race.

The reason this system is flawed is because all they can possibly test is the memory, cognitive ability and the speed at which you could do all those seemingly important problems. What we so often completely neglect is the fact that, there could be better ways of solving those same problems. What school forces us to do really, is to solve the problem in the same way as was taught and to do it as fast as possible. All the competitive exams would be so much fun if we'd given students the chance to explore possibilites about solving a problem instead of pressurizing them to them to the core about solving a bunch of problems really fast, without making any errors and doing well than the rest of the competitors. What really sucks is the fact that students find themselves lacking, if they couldn't crack a certain entrance test, when infact, it is supposed to be just a test that gets you admitted to a certain institute, but instead ends up making you believe you are stupid. Well, you can't blame the system completely. The parents are also equally responsible. I strongly believe that if the parents start to love their children unconditionally, not when they do well in some test, or crack some entrance exam, the suicide rates could go drastically down. It may not seem like it, but Indian parents specifically pressurize their children in subtle ways. This does impact mental health and is a major reason why student commit such terrible acts.


So, how do you check someone's ability?


The ability and intelligence of a person will definitely be reflected in his work, right? Let's break this down. The concept that success equals intelligence is cool, it is closely related to how intelligently a person makes certain decisions in his life. But what is more important is the fact that he his happy, with whatever he has or is been doing with his life. Imagine having lots of money, but still be unhappy. Well, when you don't have a lot of money, you might be tricked by the advertisers that more things equals more happiness, but is that true? Aren't there lots of people in this world, who have lots of good things, but are still unhappy and satisfied? Money, will obviously solve some problems, but it is not capable of filling a certain void inside you. The definition of intelligence has to change. What I believe true intelligence should be is that it is the ability of a person to observe things that happen in his life and learn from everything that he could. There are a million things in this world, of which you don't know. Out of these million things, if you could pick at-least one thing and use your creativity to explore and improve on that one thing, you are successful.
